Research Direction

My current research focuses on lightweight thermal imaging and new visual perception paradigms built around physical scene properties. I am especially interested in using long-wave infrared hyperspectral sensing to move beyond appearance-only vision toward representations that expose temperature, material emissivity, and environmental texture.

Long term, I hope to develop efficient thermal perception systems that can run with practical sensing and computation budgets while still preserving the physical interpretability needed for robotics, autonomous systems, and scientific imaging.
